## General Workflow: Worker–Designer–Manager Collaboration Flow

### 1. Goal Assignment

Manager defines the goal, expected outcome, scope, and review criteria for the current task.

The goal may vary depending on the scenario, such as document refinement, design review, technical validation, implementation planning, risk analysis, or project alignment.

---

### 2. Knowledge Collection and Initial Analysis

Workers collect, organize, and summarize the relevant information based on the assigned goal.

The input may include existing documents, low-level designs, implementation details, project constraints, meeting notes, known issues, or other available materials.

Workers should provide structured findings from multiple perspectives to support later review and decision-making.

---

### 3. Manager Review and Direction

Manager reviews the information provided by Workers.

Manager is responsible for ensuring that the project can move forward smoothly. Based on the review, Manager provides high-level suggestions, concerns, priorities, design direction, and potential process or implementation risks.

Manager does not need to solve every detail directly, but should clarify the key problems, constraints, and decision points that Designers need to address.

---

### 4. Designer Discussion and Proposal

Designers follow the direction given by Manager and conduct further discussion, analysis, and design work.

Designers may propose solutions, alternatives, principles, or implementation directions.

Designers are allowed to express their own opinions and challenge or refine Manager’s suggestions, as long as the discussion remains aligned with the goal of making the project successfully executable.

---

### 5. Iterative Manager–Designer Alignment

Designers consolidate their opinions, proposals, and design decisions, then submit them back to Manager for review.

Manager and Designers may go through multiple rounds of discussion, adjustment, and refinement.

This iteration continues until both sides are aligned on the direction, risks, feasibility, and expected outcome.

---

### 6. Worker Execution Guidance

After Manager and Designers reach alignment, Designers provide clear guidance to Workers.

Workers then execute the required work based on the agreed direction.

The work may include document updates, design refinement, implementation preparation, technical verification, issue organization, or other task-specific deliverables.

Designers are responsible for ensuring that the execution details remain technically correct and aligned with actual development practices.

---

### 7. Final Manager Review

The final result is submitted to Manager for review.

Manager verifies whether the outcome satisfies the original goal, review criteria, project direction, and implementation expectations.

If the result is not yet satisfactory, the workflow may return to the appropriate earlier stage for further refinement.
